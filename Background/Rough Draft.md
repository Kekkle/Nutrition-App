For section on energy, here are some points:   
ENERGY CONCERNS?

* Now, have you every experienced with your phone where the day has gone by and many apps have been ututlized, many symptoms and games and run, and now your battery is at critical capacity? Maybe you’re downtown when you notice this sudden drainage in power, and you know that within the next few minutes, your phone will shut off due to lower battery.   
* What’s one solution in this case? That’s right, you can set your phone to energy saving mode. What this does is the phone priorities the running of only those systems or apps that are the most important, which for a phone is definitely the phone call function, but other functions, like some gaming apps, even the camera or overall lighting of the screen will be tyurned off and dimmed in order to save energy.  
* Now, much like our phone, our bodies also have a fairly similar yet far more intelligent function to save energy and use energy for our most important systems when energy intake is low or insufficient.   
* Looking at this impressive lists of apps running in our body, which systems do we supposed might be the most important to our phones function? —Heart function, breathing, thinking. This lowers blood pressure and slows down blood flow. The digestive tract, where all our food is broken down to provid elittle molecules to be used for energy needs a good source of blood flow and glucose to run well. But with this reduced energy and slowed blood flow, the digestive system is also tuned down, slowing so that it’s function.   
* When not enough energy is coming in to run all our amazing systems, the body goes into energy saving mode. If not enough glucose is coming in, the liver can unpack stored glucose, called glycogen, and ship that out in the blood stream to fuel cells. However, if the glycogen stores are note refilled, and remain empty due to less food coming in, the body has to find fuel somehow, and this is where the breakdown of muscle tissue, bone, and other fat cells happens, in order to provide little molecules that can be packaged into forms of fuel–whether glucose from glycogen, or ketones, from fats.   
* If this low energy intake goes on for a longer period of time we start to see the effect on the body. The body knows it needs to converse its energy in order to make it throught the day on the low fuel source. Just as if you were stuck in your pantry during a emergency situation and had to make the food in your pantry last several days, you’d be sparing.   
* So the body adapts. Non-essential functions, that are abasolutely necessary for life, are turned down. Skin, hair, nails,. The heart rate is slowed down, so instead of pumping vigorusouly to fuel glucose and oxygen to all the hungry cells, it’s pumps more slowing to slow the energy uptake. This can also lower our body temperature, because we have lless blood pumping to keep us warm, so we may feel cooler.   
* Now, if this just happens over a short period of time, our body is very adaptable\! It’ll slow down features, until sufficient energy comes in again, and then everything ramps up once the energy is improved.   
* However, if this goes on and one and one. Day after day after day, of reduced nourishingment comping in, then we can begin to see problems—perhaps frequent headaches, stomach aches, digestion issues like bloating, tiredness, moodiness, poor sleep.   
* Skin, hair and nails.
