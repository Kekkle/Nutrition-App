## Section 1: Your body is like a phone (slides 1--19)

### 1A. Phones have apps

- Our phones are full of apps.
- Some are essential (phone, maps, email, browser).
- Some are helpful (calendar, camera, calculator, clock).
- Some are just for fun (Instagram, YouTube, TikTok).

### 1B. Phones need energy

- In order for all these apps to run... an energy source is needed.
- Phones need electricity.

### 1C. Your body = a phone

- Imagine your body... as a phone.
- Each system = an app.
- Just like apps on a phone, your body has systems that do different jobs.
- Some body systems are vital to your survival (brain, lungs, heart, intestines, stomach, bones, blood).
- Some less so (skin/hair, reproductive, nails).

### 1D. Bodies need energy too

- In order for all those systems to run... an energy source is needed.
- While our bodies require: food, sleep, water...
- The main source of energy is... food!
- Food = energy.

### 1E. Three categories of food

- All food falls under three categories:
  1. Carbohydrates
  2. Proteins
  3. Fats
- These three categories are called **macronutrients** = nutrients we need in BIG amounts.

## Section 2: Meet the macronutrients (slides 20--40)

### 2A. Identify foods by macro

- Let's take a closer look at each macronutrient...
- **Carbohydrates** -- Can you come up with some examples? (bread, pasta, banana, broccoli, potatoes, cookies, rice, oats, cereal, fruits, vegetables, cake)
- **Protein** -- Ideas of protein foods? (chicken, sausage, cheese, milk, yogurt, nuts, salmon, steak, eggs, beans)
- **Fat** -- What foods might those be? (butter, coconut oil, olive oil, pistachios, nuts, avocado)

### 2B. What decides a food's category?

- But what decides whether a food is a "protein" or a "carb" or a "fat"?
- It comes down to what they're mostly made of.
- Foods are made of lots of different molecules, but we classify them based on what they have the most of.

### 2C. The molecules behind each macro

- **Carbohydrates** are made of sugar molecules called glucose, fructose, and galactose.
- **Proteins** are long chains of molecules called amino acids.
- **Fats** are made of fatty acid + glycerol units (called lipids).

### 2D. Most foods are a mix

- A food is labeled by what it has most of... but most foods are a mix.
- **Example -- steak:** A steak has mostly protein molecules (amino acids)... but it also has some fat. So although steak is a source of both protein and fat, it is primarily a protein as it is mostly made up of amino acids.
- **Example -- beans:** Beans have all three macronutrients. That's right, carbohydrates! Beans are primarily a carbohydrate source although they also provide some protein.
- **Example -- fruits:** Fruits are primarily a carbohydrate as only tiny amounts of protein and fat are present.
- **Pure-macro foods:** Some foods are only just one macronutrient as only one molecule is present.
  - Honey = carbs
  - Olive oil = pure fat
  - Egg whites = mostly protein

## Section 3: How food becomes energy -- cells first (slides 41--67)

### 3A. Transition: from macros to energy

- Now that you understand the three macronutrients...
- Let's explore how exactly... food → energy.
- How does food turn into energy? From a potato chip... to real power inside your body.

### 3B. But first -- what are we made of?

- Hmm... wait a second...
- To better understand this, let's first take a closer look... at our body and what we are made of.
- Just like food is made of tiny particles... our bodies are made of something tiny too.
- **Cells!**

### 3C. Cells 101

- Every part of your body is made of tiny units called cells.
- Cells → tissues → organs → body systems (hierarchy).
- Not all cells are the same! Different cells have different jobs.
  - Skin cells: provide protection for the body.
  - Stomach cells: help break down food.
  - Brain cells: send messages to the body.

### 3D. Cells need energy

- In order to do their jobs, cells need energy.
- Much like a phone.

### 3E. Carb digestion pathway

- Now we're ready to explore how food turns into energy!
- Let's first examine carbs...
- When we take a bite of a carbohydrate...
  1. The carb travels from our mouth to our stomach.
  2. It is broken down by stomach acid into smaller pieces for our small intestine.
  3. Once in the small intestine, carb particles are broken down further into tiny glucose molecules.
  4. Glucose is then absorbed and transported to the bloodstream.
  5. Glucose travels in your blood... looking for cells that need energy.

## Section 4: Insulin -- the key that unlocks cells (slides 68--83)

### 4A. Glucose is the main fuel

- Glucose is the main source of energy... for every single cell.

### 4B. How glucose gets into cells

- So how does glucose get into cells?
- Glucose in the blood... signals to the pancreas (a little organ).
- The pancreas releases a hormone called... insulin.
- Insulin joins glucose in the bloodstream, ready to help needy cells.

### 4C. Insulin is like a key

- Insulin is like a key.
- It opens a portal on the cell... to allow glucose to enter.
- (Visual: multiple cells being unlocked by insulin, glucose flowing in, batteries filling up.)

### 4D. Glucose becomes energy inside the cell

- Once glucose enters the cell, it gets turned into... energy!
- Which helps the cells to perform their functions:
  - Brain cells to send signals to the body (I'm a neuron!)
  - Bone cells to grow and repair (I'm an osteocyte!)
  - Intestinal cells to break down and absorb food (We're epithelial cells!)
  - Skin cells to provide a strong barrier of protection for your body (We're cells of the epidermis! That's the outer layer of skin.)
- (Summary visual: food → molecules → cell → organs → body systems → body.)

## Section 5: Why all three macros matter + fat digestion (slides 84--99)

### 5A. The big question

- You might be wondering: If we get our energy from carbs, why do we even need fats and protein?
- All food falls under three categories: Carbohydrates, Proteins, Fats.
- Carbs = energy. Proteins = energy + building blocks. Fats = energy + other essential roles.

### 5B. Fat digestion pathway

- Let's now take a look at fats...
- Remember how most foods are made of more than one macronutrient?
- When we eat food containing fats, they often contain carbohydrates as well.
- Like this potato chip... (magnifying glass shows carb hexagons and fat lipids).
- So when we eat food containing fats...
  1. The food travels from our mouth to our stomach.
  2. It is broken down by stomach acid into smaller pieces for our small intestine.
  3. Once in the small intestine, the food is broken down further.
  4. The carb portion breaks down into tiny glucose molecules.
  5. And the portion containing fats... into tiny fat droplets called lipids.
  6. Glucose is then absorbed and transported to the bloodstream.
  7. And the lipids are absorbed and transported to the lymph system.
- The lymph system is another set of tiny tubes in the body that helps move certain nutrients, like fats, before they enter the bloodstream.
