# App Name Ideas

Names to research and test. Each is meant to sound fun and a little odd — memorable like "Wordle" — while linking back to the app's nutrition and body-science themes.

## Availability Research (March 2026)

| #  | Name      | Why it works | Taken? | Notes |
|----|-----------|--------------|--------|-------|
| 1  | Munchii   | "Munch" with a cute, app-ready ending. Instantly food-related, ties to "Macro Munch!" quiz. | Likely clear | No exact "Munchii" match. Several "Munch" apps exist (Munch AI, Munchi restaurant tool, Munch social foodie app) but none use this exact spelling. |
| 2  | Grubble   | "Grub" (slang for food) + bouncy "-ble." Sounds like a friendly creature. | Conflict | "Gruble" is a construction logistics SaaS. "Grubbl" is a restaurant discovery app. Very close in name and food-adjacent. |
| 3  | Noomph    | "Nourish" + "oomph." That burst of energy good food gives you. | Likely clear | No direct match. "Oomph" is a fitness/nutrition app but "Noomph" itself appears unused. |
| 4  | Chomple   | From "chomp." Oddly satisfying to say. Mascot-ready. | Clear | No app or company found using this name. "Chomp" and "Chimple" exist but are different enough. |
| 5  | Blorb     | Goofy nonsense echoing "absorb" — what the body does with nutrients. | Likely clear | "Blorb" is a niche file format for interactive fiction games, not a consumer app or brand. No conflict for an education app. |
| 6  | Niblo     | Squished "nibble." Short, strange, sticky. | Taken | "Niblo" (getniblo.com) is an AI nutrition tracking app. Direct conflict in the nutrition space. |
| 7  | Gutsy     | "Gut" (digestion) + gutsy (bold/brave). Double meaning for empowerment. | Taken | Multiple companies: a women's fitness app, a cybersecurity startup, and a software dev company all use "Gutsy." |
| 8  | Nomble    | "Nom" (internet eating sound) + "-ble." Instantly food, instantly fun. | Conflict | "Nomble" (nomble.app / nomble.dev) is a software development company. Name is actively in use. |
| 9  | Morsl     | Stripped-down "morsel." Small bites of food and knowledge. | Taken | "Morsl" is an Australian workplace micro-market company AND an AI recipe app (morslapp.com). Both in the food space. |
| 10 | Glucki    | "Glucose" made adorable. The body's go-to fuel in a party hat. | Conflict | glucki.com sells children's products. "GLUKI" (glukiinc.com) is a Type 1 Diabetes education app for kids — very close in audience and health/education space. |
| 11 | Snazzle   | "Snack" crashed into "dazzle." Sparkly and silly. | Conflict | Multiple "Snazzle" brands: Indian clothing e-commerce, UK shoe retailer, open-source Scratch frontend, and snazzle.net "launching soon." |
| 12 | Fuelix    | "Fuel" + "-ix" (sounds like Felix). Your body's lucky energy friend. | Taken | Fuelix / Fuel iX is an established enterprise AI platform with 50,000+ users and a mobile app. |
| 13 | Noshii    | From "nosh" (to eat/snack casually). Cute and approachable. | Likely clear | No exact "Noshii" match. Several "Nosh" apps exist (delivery, meal planning) but none use this spelling. |
| 14 | Cellix    | "Cell" + techy "-ix." Where the energy magic happens inside the body. | Taken | Cellix is a biotech/microfluidics company (est. 2006, acquired by Randox Laboratories in 2023). |
| 15 | Gobbo     | From "gobble." Chunky, silly, cartoon sidekick energy. | Likely clear | "Gobbo" is a common Italian surname used by several Italian industrial companies (construction, tires, plastics). No app or tech product uses the name. |
| 16 | Juvo      | From Latin *juvare* (to help/nourish). Short, unexpected, cool. | Taken | Juvo is an established fintech company (est. 2014) with mobile apps, processing 100,000+ loans/day. |

## Summary: Cleanest Names

These names had no direct conflicts or only very distant, non-competing uses:

- **Chomple** — completely clear
- **Blorb** — clear (only a niche file format, no consumer brand)
- **Munchii** — likely clear (similar "Munch" names exist but not this exact spelling)
- **Noomph** — likely clear (no match found)
- **Noshii** — likely clear (similar "Nosh" names exist but not this spelling)
- **Gobbo** — likely clear (only Italian industrial firms, no tech/app use)

## Names to Avoid

These have direct conflicts, especially in food, health, or education:

- **Niblo** — taken by an AI nutrition app (direct competitor overlap)
- **Morsl** — taken in the food space twice over
- **Glucki** — conflict with a kids' diabetes education app (too close in audience)
- **Fuelix** — taken by a large enterprise AI company
- **Cellix** — taken by a biotech company
- **Juvo** — taken by an established fintech
- **Gutsy** — taken by multiple companies including a women's fitness app
