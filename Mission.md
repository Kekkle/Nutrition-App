# Mission statment and vision:

 To provide an engaging and scientifically accurate tool-kit for adolescent girls that enhances their understanding of the interaction between nutrition and female physiology and enables them to make powerful dietary and lifestyle choices during the formative years that will support them into a healthier, stronger adulthood. 

# Our Vision:

 To empower adolescent girls with accurate nutrition information so that disordered eating is an easily bypassed detour on the journey through life.

Here’s how I’ll apply this *going forward* (so you know what to expect from me):

### **How this will influence future output**

* **Tone & framing**  
  * Empowering, not prescriptive  
  * Curious and capability-building, not rule-based  
  * Avoids moral language around food (“good/bad,” “clean/junk”)  
* **Gamification choices**  
  * Focus on **function, energy, and choice**, not restriction  
  * Systems thinking (how the body uses food) over calorie counting  
  * Visualizing *support* and *capacity*, not punishment or failure  
* **Nutrition explanations**  
  * Scientifically accurate but **non-triggering**  
  * Emphasize *why* the body needs things, not *what to avoid*  
  * Ultra-processed foods framed as “less supportive,” not “forbidden”  
* **Audience lens**  
  * Specifically adolescent girls and **female physiology**, when relevant  
  * Respectful of developmental stage (physical \+ emotional)  
  * Designed to make disordered eating feel unnecessary, not tempting
