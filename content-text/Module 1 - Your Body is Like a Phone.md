# Module 1: Your Body is Like a Phone

---

## Node 1.1: Sort the apps

### Lesson panels

1. Phones are full of apps. You tap them to call people, take photos, play games, and more.
2. Some apps feel essential. Phone, Camera, and Messages help you stay connected and safe.
3. Other apps are mostly for fun — like games, photo filters, or short videos.
4. Many apps can run at once. Some stay open quietly in the background.
5. Your body is similar. It runs lots of jobs at the same time — not on a screen, but inside you.
6. Let's practice! Sort these phone apps into essential ones and fun ones.

### Game: "Sort the apps"
- Type: Drag and drop
- Prompt: Drag each app into Essential or Fun.
- Items: Phone, Camera, Instagram, Games, Messages, TikTok
- Essential: Phone, Camera, Messages
- Fun: Instagram, Games, TikTok

### Fun fact
Your phone typically runs about 30 apps. Your body runs way more systems than that — all at the same time!

---

## Node 1.2: Charge the phone

### Lesson panels

1. All those apps need energy. Without power, nothing on the phone can wake up.
2. If the battery is totally dead, the screen stays black. Taps do nothing.
3. So we plug the phone in and let it charge. That refills the battery.
4. Electricity is what flows in. It is the phone's power source.
5. When the battery is full enough, every app can work again.
6. Quick check: what actually powers a phone?

### Game: "Charge the phone"
- Type: Multiple choice
- Prompt: What powers a phone?
- Options: Electricity (correct), Food, Sleep, Water

### Fun fact
A fully charged phone battery can last about 10-12 hours. Your body needs to recharge with food, water, and sleep every single day!

## Node 1.3: Build the body

### Lesson panels

1. Now picture your body like one big phone. Each major part has a big job.
2. Your brain is like a control center. It helps you think, feel, and decide.
3. Your heart pumps blood like a steady drumbeat. It never takes a day off.
4. Your lungs pull in air and swap fresh oxygen for used-up air you breathe out.
5. Muscles help you move. Your digestive system breaks down food so your body can use it.
6. Brain, heart, lungs, muscles, digestion — all running together, right now.

### Game: "Build the body"
- Type: Drag and drop
- Prompt: Drag each part to the right spot on the body.
- Items: Brain, Heart, Lungs, Muscles, Stomach
- Brain -> Head, Heart -> Chest (left), Lungs -> Chest (right), Muscles -> Arms, Stomach -> Belly

### Fun fact
Your brain uses about 20% of all the energy your body makes — even though it's only about 2% of your body weight!

## Node 1.4: Fill the battery

### Lesson panels

1. If your body is like a phone, it still needs fuel. It cannot run on electricity from the wall.
2. Food is your main everyday energy source. It helps you think, move, and grow.
3. Water does not give energy like food, but it keeps blood and nutrients moving smoothly.
4. Sleep is recovery time. Your body repairs, sorts memories, and gets ready for tomorrow.
5. Food, water, and sleep together refill your inner battery — day after day.
6. Drag each picture into the bucket that matches. Does it power your body, or not?

### Game: "Fill the battery"
- Type: Drag and drop
- Prompt: Sort what powers your body from what does not.
- Powers your body: Apple, Water bottle, Pillow
- Does not power your body: Battery, Lightning bolt, Wi-Fi signal

### Fun fact
Sleep is when your body does most of its repair work. Your muscles grow, your brain organizes memories, and your immune system recharges — all while you snooze!

## Node 1.5: Mystery boxes

### Lesson panels

1. Food is your body's main fuel. You need it to play, learn, and heal.
2. But not every food is built the same way. An apple, bread, and yogurt all do different jobs.
3. Scientists group foods by the big nutrients inside them. Those groups help us talk about how food supports you.
4. There are three main types your body needs in large amounts. Three big buckets — not two, not ten.
5. Soon you will open those boxes and meet carbs, protein, and fats. For now, just count the groups.
6. So — how many main energy groups does food come in?

### Game: "Mystery boxes"
- Type: Multiple choice
- Prompt: Food comes in how many main energy groups?
- Options: 2, 3 (correct), 5, 10

### Fun fact
The three groups are called macronutrients: carbohydrates, proteins, and fats. "Macro" means big — because your body needs these in large amounts!
