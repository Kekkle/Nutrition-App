# Module 1: Meet Celly

---

## Node 1.1: Sort the apps

### Lesson panels

1. Celly is a phone full of apps.
2. Some apps feel essential. Phone, Camera, and Messages help you stay connected and safe.
3. Other apps are mostly for fun — like games, photo filters, or short videos.
4. Many apps can run at once, while some stay open quietly in the background.
5. Your body is kinda similar. It runs lots of jobs at the same time — not on a screen, but inside you.

### Game: "Sort the apps"
- Type: Drag and drop
- Prompt: Drag each app into Essential or Fun.
- Items: Phone, Camera, Instagram, Games, Messages, TikTok
- Essential: Phone, Camera, Messages, Clock, Maps, Calendar
- Fun: Instagram, Tik Tok, Games, Musik, Shopping, YouTube

### Fun fact
A phone typically runs about 30 apps, but your body runs way more systems than that — all at the same time!

## Node 1.2: Charge the phone

### Lesson panels

1. All those apps need energy. Without power, the phone screen stays black. 
2. Plugging in the phone and letting it charge refills the battery.
3. Electricity is the phone's power source.
4. When the battery is full enough, every app can work again.

### Game: "Charge the phone"
- Type: Drag and drop
- Prompt: Power up your phone!
- Options: Drag the electrical plug to the socket and connect to the phone.

### Fun fact
A fully charged phone battery can last about 10-12 hours.

## Node 1.3: Build the body

### Lesson panels

1. Your body has many "apps" running all the time!
2. Your brain is like a control center. It helps you think, feel, and decide.
3. Your heart pumps blood like a steady drumbeat. It never takes a day off.
4. Your lungs pull in air and swap fresh oxygen for used-up air you breathe out.
5. Your digestive system breaks down and absorbs food.
6. Bones give your body a structure, and muscles help you move your body. 
6. Brain, heart, lungs, bones, muscles, digestion and more — all functioning together, right now.

### Game: "Build the body"
- Type: Drag and drop
- Prompt: Drag each part to the right spot on the body form.
- Items: Brain, Heart, Lungs, Muscles, Stomach
- Brain -> Head, Heart -> Chest (left), Lungs -> Chest (right), Muscles -> Arms, Stomach -> Belly

### Fun fact
Your brain uses about 20% of all the energy your body makes — even though it's only about 2% of your body weight!

4. Water does not give energy like food, but it keeps blood and nutrients moving smoothly.
5. Sleep is recovery time. Your body repairs, sorts memories, and gets ready for tomorrow.

Sleep is when your body does most of its repair work. Your muscles grow, your brain organizes memories, and your immune system recharges — all while you snooze!
